Introduction

Honeypots are an essential tool in cybersecurity, designed to attract attackers and gather intelligence on their techniques and methods. By simulating real systems, honeypots serve as decoys to lure malicious actors, enabling security teams to study and respond to threats effectively.

This page provides an overview of what honeypots are, their types, and their benefits and risks.

Types of Honeypots

Pure Honeypots

These honeypots are isolated and used exclusively for research, focusing on capturing detailed information about attacker behaviors.

Low-Interaction Honeypots

These simulate basic systems to detect automated attacks like scanning or probing. They are easy to deploy but offer limited insight into advanced threats.

High-Interaction Honeypots

These provide a realistic environment for attackers, enabling detailed analysis of sophisticated threats. However, they require significant resources and careful isolation.

Benefits of Honeypots

  • Threat Intelligence: Honeypots provide insights into attacker methods and strategies.
  • Early Detection: They can identify threats before they affect production systems.
  • Cost-Effective: Honeypots are a low-cost method to test cybersecurity defenses.

Risks of Honeypots

  • Exploitation: If not isolated, attackers can use honeypots to further compromise networks.
  • Detection: Advanced attackers might identify honeypots, reducing their effectiveness.
  • Resource Intensity: High-interaction honeypots require significant effort to maintain.
